A day that keeps circling one idea — the hard part of AI has moved from generating to verifying. Snorkel's Senior SWE-Bench grades coding agents on deliberately under-specified tasks with a per-solution validation agent, targeting the judgment junior-vs-senior benchmarks never measured; the Act2Answer benchmark shows vision-language-action robot models lose 20-40 points of world knowledge after fine-tuning to act, unless they keep doing VQA alongside; and 'The Gauge Broke' revisits METR's RCT (16 devs, 246 tasks) where developers felt ~20% faster with AI but measured ~19% slower, corroborated by Faros AI's 10k-dev finding of PRs merged +98% but review time +91%. Godot bans AI-authored code contributions for the same reason — review capacity is the fixed constraint — while Cloudflare's Sept 15 policy forces AI crawlers to split search from training or pay. On the build side, HF+Cerebras ship a modular open voice pipeline (Parakeet + Gemma 4 31B on Cerebras + Qwen3TTS) built to kill P95 tail latency, already on 9,000+ Reachy Mini robots. One to watch: Anthropic's Claude Science — a workbench, not a new model, betting scientific AI's edge is an auditable, reproducible workflow.
