A spread day with one quiet thread — capability showing up somewhere other than raw model size — plus the memory shortage turning into real money. Meta's Brain2Qwerty v2 decodes typed text from non-invasive MEG at 61% word accuracy (78% for the best participant), up from ~8% for prior non-invasive methods, with accuracy improving log-linearly with data; a paper argues a 35B agent (Agents-A1) matches trillion-parameter models by 'scaling the horizon' — making the intermediate agent process trainable rather than growing the model. An open-weights release day: Meituan's LongCat-2.0 (1.6T total / ~48B active MoE, the stealth 'owl-alpha' on OpenRouter), DeepSeek V4 merged into llama.cpp for local runs, and DeepReinforce's self-scaffolding Ornith-1.0 (MIT). Industry: a Ramp/Revelio report finds high-intensity AI adopters grew headcount 10.2% (entry-level +12%) even as Goldman ties AI to ~16k net jobs lost/month — selection-skewed and 'messier' either way; and Anthropic signs a discounted-Claude deal with California while the federal government brands it a 'supply-chain risk.' One to watch: South Korea's $550B 'RAMageddon' memory buildout against a US DRAM price-fixing suit.
