Why your model can't read a million words at once — and the trick that lets it pretend

Self-Attention — 2026-06-13

Weekly Deep Dive · 2026-06-13

This week MiniMax shipped a production model built around their own sparse-attention mechanism, pitching million-token context without the runaway cost. We use that as the doorway to teach how long-context attention actually works: why attention is quadratic, why the KV cache is the real deployment bottleneck, and how "sparse attention" — a cheap scout that picks a small set of context blocks to actually read — escapes both costs. We trace the lineage from Native Sparse Attention (NSA) into this week's models, and we read the receipts honestly: real but sub-theoretical speedups, on-par (not better) quality on the tested tasks, and the blind-spot risk on diffuse queries that standard needle-in-a-haystack benchmarks don't catch.
