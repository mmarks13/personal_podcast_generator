From control theory to six-times throughput: inside Mamba and state-space models

Self-Attention — 2026-06-20

Weekly Deep Dive · 2026-06-20

Nvidia's Nemotron 3 Ultra uses a "hybrid Mamba-Attention" architecture — but what is Mamba? This episode teaches state-space models from first principles: why transformer attention's quadratic cost becomes painful at long contexts, how the classical SSM formulation from control theory offers a linear-time alternative, what made prior SSMs (including S4) fail at selective memory tasks, and the two innovations Mamba introduced — input-dependent selectivity and the hardware-efficient parallel scan — that changed the picture. We cover the Mamba-3B-beats-Transformer-6B result, the real failure modes (in-context learning, vision tasks), why hybrid architectures work and what the right attention-to-SSM ratio looks like, and what the Mamba-2 state-space-duality paper means for the field.
