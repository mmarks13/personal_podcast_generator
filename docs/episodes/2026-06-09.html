Daily AI Briefing — 2026-06-09

A day of big capability claims meeting pointed questions about whether they pay off. Anthropic launched Claude Fable 5 (with a restricted sibling, Mythos 5), and Xiaomi reported the first trillion-parameter model to cross 1,000 tokens/sec decode. Meanwhile Apple revealed an Apple Intelligence architecture built on Google Gemini models, a Microsoft open-source supply-chain attack targeted AI developer tooling, and a widely-shared analysis questioned the industry's economics — echoing a research benchmark arguing benchmark gains haven't yet translated into real-world deployment.

Papers

  • Agents' Last Exam (ALE) — a benchmark for long-horizon, economically valuable, real-world agent tasks with verifiable outcomes; argues the benchmark-to-deployment gap is an evaluation problem.
  • FlashMemory-DeepSeek-V4: Lookahead Sparse Attention — proactively predicts which KV-cache chunks matter for ultra-long context, keeping only query-critical chunks in GPU memory.
  • End-to-End Context Compression at Scale — compresses long context to tame KV-cache growth, compatible with production inference engines.
  • Why Muon Outperforms Adam: A Curvature Perspective — explains Muon's ~2× training-efficiency edge over Adam via a second-order/curvature view of the loss landscape.

Releases

  • Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 — Anthropic's new flagship; same underlying model, different safeguards. Claims SOTA on most tested benchmarks, millions-of-tokens context; pricing $10/M input, $50/M output; Fable 5 GA now. Company-reported examples: Stripe 50M-line Ruby migration, ~10× drug-design acceleration.
  • System Card: Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 (PDF) — reports low misaligned-behavior rate (similar to Opus 4.8); zero harmful single-turn cyberattacks succeeded in testing.
  • Xiaomi MiMo-V2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed — 1T-parameter MoE reporting ~1,000–1,200 tokens/sec decode on a single 8-GPU node via FP4 quantization, DFlash speculative decoding, and the TileRT engine; FP4 weights released on Hugging Face.

Industry & Discussion

  • Apple reveals new AI architecture built around Google Gemini models (MacRumors) — Apple Intelligence built on foundation models co-developed with Google using Gemini; runs on-device and via Private Cloud Compute; adds image generation, photo editing, visual Q&A, and a system orchestrator. No device requirements, prices, or firm dates given.
  • Microsoft's open-source tools were hacked to steal passwords of AI developers (TechCrunch) — ≥70 Microsoft GitHub repos disabled after credential-stealing malware injection; many tied to Azure and AI dev tooling (Claude Code, Gemini CLI, VS Code). Second incident in weeks; possible re-compromise of the mid-May Durable Task project.
  • AI Is Slowing Down (Ed Zitron, opinion/analysis) — argues infrastructure spend (e.g., OpenAI's projected $50B 2026 compute, ~$80–100B/GW data centers) far outruns realistic revenue; notes token-based billing prompting enterprise spend caps. Bearish single-author analysis — treat figures accordingly.
