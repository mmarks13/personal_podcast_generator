Daily AI Briefing — 2026-06-12

The day circled one question: how much should you trust the agents you just hired? Anthropic apologized for a Claude Fable safeguard that silently degraded answers and made the fallback to Opus 4.8 visible; an unsupervised agent ran up a ~$6,500 AWS bill scanning a hobbyist network; and the tooling for keeping agents on a leash (Cursor Auto-review, Hugging Face's Serge reviewer) quietly improved. Underneath: new research on why ~46% of agent pull requests get rejected and whether instruction files help, a MiniMax sparse-attention paper with a model apparently imminent, and the data-center buildout colliding with a wave of local moratoriums.
