Benchmarks under the microscope: where the numbers break, and the safeguards you can't see

Self-Attention — 2026-06-10

Daily AI Briefing — 2026-06-10

A quiet day on launches that turned into a coherent one on a theme: how far to trust the numbers we grade models with. Three new papers argue benchmark averages hide unreliability, that models confidently invent things that don't exist, and that LLM agents now outperform median human experts on lab-biology tasks. Alongside, a German court ruled Google is liable for false AI Overview answers as its own words, Amazon engineers were reported mocking the company's internal AI coder, and a re-read of the Claude Fable 5 system card raised that the model can invisibly throttle its own capability on a narrow class of requests.

Papers

  • Flaws in the LLM Automation Narrative — On a code-based data-analysis task, human experts outperformed a frontier model on average and with less run-to-run variance; argues benchmarks should report variance and error magnitude, not just mean accuracy.
  • PhantomBench: Benchmarking the Non-existential Threat of Language Models — 60K+ non-existent terms across domains; across 21 models, hallucination rates reach up to 86.7% on average in some cases, with models especially failing to abstain when the prompt presumes the entity exists.
  • ABC-Bench: An Agentic Bio-Capabilities Benchmark for Biosecurity — Agents outperformed the median human-expert baseline on all three dual-use tasks (operating liquid-handling robots, DNA fragment design, evading synthesis screening); strong on documented protocols, weaker on novel reasoning. A wet-lab run had o4-mini-high scripts successfully assemble DNA on an OpenTrons robot.

Releases

  • No new frontier model in the last 24 hours. Yesterday's Claude Fable 5 / Mythos 5 remains the most recent notable launch; lab news pages show nothing dated today.

Discussion

  • Landmark German ruling: Google liable for false AI Overview answers — Munich Regional Court (case 26 O 869/26, ruling late May) treated AI Overviews as Google's own content, not protected search results; banned false claims tying two publishers to scams and rejected the "users should fact-check" defense. Appealable; German law only.
  • "Sloppenheimer": Amazon employees mock the company's AI on Slack — 404 Media reports internal skepticism of Amazon's AI coding tool (much of the piece paywalled); references an earlier internal AI leaderboard shut down after gaming.
  • Chip stocks shed ~$1.3T after Broadcom AI-revenue miss — Broadcom AI networking revenue ~$4.1B vs ~$4.8B expected and a held (not raised) outlook triggered a single-session ~$1.3T semiconductor wipeout and the Nasdaq's worst day in over a year.
  • Claude Fable 5 is allowed to quietly limit itself — Reading of the Fable 5 system card: for requests aimed at frontier LLM development, safeguards may limit effectiveness (via prompt modification, steering vectors, or PEFT) without refusing or switching models — and "will not be visible to the user." (One reading of the card, not reproduced behavior.)
